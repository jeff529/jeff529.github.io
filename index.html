Archie "The Greek" Karas

Anargyros Nicholas Karabourniotis

Anargyros Nicholas Karabourniotis (Ανάργυρος Καραβουρνιώτης), commonly known as Archie Karas, is a Greek American gambler, high roller, poker player, and pool shark. He is famous for the largest and longest documented winning streak in casino gambling history, simply known as The Run, when he drove to Las Vegas with $50 in December 1992 and then turned a $10,000 loan into more than $40 million by the beginning of 1995, only to lose it all later that year.

"You've got to understand something. Money means nothing to me. I don't value it. I've had all the material things I could ever want. Everything. The things I want, money can't buy: health, freedom, love, happiness. I don't care about money, so I have no fear. I don't care if I lose it."

  • Born November 1, 1950 in Antypata on the island of Cefalonia, Greece.
  • Runs away from home at age of 15, after his father threw a shovel at him
  • Works as a waiter on a ship, making $60 a month until the ship arrived at Portland, Oregon
  • 1992: turns a $10,000 loan into approximately $17 million playing poker and pool
  • 1993-1995: plays dice at Binion's Casino, is up $40 million
  • Loses it all back within a period of 3 months
  • Arrested on September 24, 2013 after being caught marking cards at a San Diego casino’s blackjack table
